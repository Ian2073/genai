# Thumbelina

Author: Hans Christian Andersen

Once upon a time, a woman who wanted a child asked a witch for help. The witch gave her a special seed. When planted, it grew into a flower, and inside was a tiny girl no bigger than a thumb. The woman named her Thumbelina.

One night, a toad saw Thumbelina sleeping and decided she would make a perfect wife for her son. The toad took Thumbelina to a lily pad in the middle of a stream. When Thumbelina woke up, she was terrified.

Fish in the stream felt sorry for her and nibbled through the lily pad's stem, letting it float away with Thumbelina. She drifted downstream until a beetle found her and carried her to his tree. But the other beetles thought she was ugly, so the beetle let her go.

All summer and autumn, Thumbelina lived alone in the forest. When winter came, she was cold and hungry. She knocked on the door of a field mouse, who kindly took her in.

The field mouse had a rich neighbor, a mole. The mouse wanted Thumbelina to marry him. Thumbelina didn't want to live underground forever, but she had no choice.

In the mole's tunnel, Thumbelina found an injured swallow. She cared for him all winter. When spring came, the swallow recovered and offered to take Thumbelina away with him. But she felt she couldn't leave the kind field mouse.

As her wedding day approached, Thumbelina went outside to say goodbye to the sun. The swallow appeared again! "Come with me!" he said. This time, Thumbelina agreed.

The swallow flew with her to a warm country with beautiful flowers. He set her down on a flower, where she met the flower prince, who was just her size. He fell in love with her immediately.

"Will you marry me and be Queen of the Flowers?" he asked. Thumbelina said yes! She received beautiful wings and a new name: Maia. She lived happily ever after in the land of flowers.

Moral: Kindness is rewarded, and everyone will find where they truly belong.
